# GST Checklist — Ghar Pahuch Seva

> How GST applies to a 25%-commission service marketplace, plus a filing routine so you
> never miss a deadline. **Not tax advice** — confirm specifics with a local CA.

---

## Do you need to register now?

- **Mandatory** once your turnover crosses **₹20 lakh/year** (services, normal-category states).
- At ~₹1.5L/month commission income you cross this around **month 5–6**.
- **Recommendation: register preemptively.** It's free, takes ~30 min, and avoids the
  scramble (and penalty) of crossing the threshold unregistered.
- Registration is also required to switch Razorpay to **live mode** smoothly and to issue
  proper tax invoices.

## How GST applies to your model

- You are an **intermediary / marketplace operator**. GST applies to **your commission**,
  not the worker's full service charge.
- **Commission GST rate: 18%.**
- Example on a ₹1,000 service:
  - Customer pays ₹1,000.
  - Your commission (25%) = ₹250.
  - GST on commission (18% of ₹250) = ₹45 → paid to government.
  - Worker gets ₹750 (workers below ₹20L turnover are typically unregistered).
- **TCS note:** E-commerce operators may be required to collect **TCS (1%)** on the net
  value of taxable supplies by sellers through the platform. Confirm applicability with
  your CA based on your structure (proprietorship vs registered partnership) before launch.

## Registration checklist (one-time)

- [ ] PAN of the business/proprietor
- [ ] Aadhaar of the proprietor/partners
- [ ] Photograph (proprietor/partners)
- [ ] Proof of business address (electricity bill / rent agreement + NOC)
- [ ] Bank account proof (cancelled cheque / statement / passbook)
- [ ] UDYAM/MSME certificate (helpful, not mandatory)
- [ ] Digital signature (for company/LLP) or Aadhaar OTP (for proprietorship)
- [ ] Register at https://www.gst.gov.in → "New Registration"
- [ ] Save your **GSTIN** once issued (you'll add it to Razorpay + invoices)

## Monthly / quarterly filing routine

> Most small businesses opt for the **QRMP scheme** (Quarterly Return, Monthly Payment) —
> file returns quarterly but pay tax monthly. Confirm which scheme you're on.

| Return | What it is | Frequency | Due (typical) |
|--------|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| **GSTR-1** | Outward supplies (your commission invoices) | Monthly or Quarterly (QRMP) | 11th (monthly) / 13th (quarterly) |
| **GSTR-3B** | Summary return + tax payment | Monthly or Quarterly (QRMP) | 20th (monthly) / 22nd–24th (quarterly) |
| **PMT-06** | Monthly tax payment (if on QRMP) | Monthly (months 1 & 2 of quarter) | 25th |
| **GSTR-9** | Annual return | Yearly | 31 Dec (next FY) |

> Exact dates shift; always confirm on the GST portal for your state.

### Set these phone reminders
- [ ] **10th of each month** — compile last month's bookings & commission total
- [ ] **11th** — file GSTR-1 (or prep for quarterly)
- [ ] **20th** — file GSTR-3B & pay tax
- [ ] **End of financial year** — GSTR-9 annual return

**Late filing penalty:** ~₹50/day (₹20/day for nil returns) + 18% interest on unpaid tax.
Don't skip nil months — file even when revenue is zero.

---

## Monthly habit (5-minute routine)

1. Export the month's completed bookings from your admin/database.
2. Sum the **commission** (25% column) — that's your taxable turnover.
3. Compute 18% GST on it.
4. File GSTR-1, then GSTR-3B, and pay.
5. Save the filed return PDF in a "GST" folder (keep records 6+ years).

---

## Records to keep (6+ years)

- [ ] All commission/tax invoices issued
- [ ] Monthly booking exports (proof of turnover)
- [ ] Razorpay settlement statements
- [ ] Filed GSTR-1 / GSTR-3B confirmations
- [ ] Worker payout records (75% payments)

Keeping clean monthly records is what makes year-end (and any audit) painless. Hand these
to a CA quarterly if you'd rather not file yourself — ~₹500–1,000/month is well spent
once you're past the first few months.
